About Botswana

Discover why Botswana is considered one of Africa's premier safari destinations

The Jewel of Southern Africa

Botswana is a landlocked country in Southern Africa known for its pristine wilderness areas. Approximately 70% of its territory is covered by the Kalahari Desert, yet it boasts some of the most spectacular wildlife viewing opportunities on the continent.

From the vast salt pans of Makgadikgadi to the lush waterways of the Okavango Delta, Botswana offers diverse ecosystems that support an incredible array of wildlife, including the Big Five.

With a strong commitment to conservation and sustainable tourism, Botswana provides exclusive, low-impact safari experiences that benefit both visitors and local communities.

Botswana Cuisine

Seswaa

National Dish

Bogobe

Tswana Staple

Thick porridge made from sorghum or maize meal, served with meat stews or vegetables as the foundation of most meals.

Serobe

Tswana Staple

Tswana Staple

Wildlife & Conservation

Encounter Africa's most iconic animals in their natural habitat

Protecting Botswana's Natural Heritage

Botswana is home to an incredible diversity of wildlife, including large populations of elephants, lions, leopards, cheetahs, and endangered African wild dogs.

The country has implemented successful conservation strategies that have made it a safe haven for many species. Community-based conservation programs ensure that local people benefit from wildlife tourism.

From the desert-adapted species of the Kalahari to the water-loving antelope of the Okavango Delta, Botswana offers unique wildlife viewing opportunities throughout the year.
